Clockwork Counselling

Counselling for adults offered via video, telephone, or face to face in Gosforth, Newcastle upon Tyne.

A free telephone consultation (up to 15 minutes) can be arranged to discuss your needs, answer any questions you may have, and help you decide whether you wish to go ahead with counselling sessions.

Joanne Park BACP Registered and Accredited Counsellor

My experience as a counsellor began when I qualified in 2010. I have gained experience in a variety of settings including the NHS, mental health charities and employee assistance programmes (EAP). I have also worked in the charity sector since 2006 facilitating mental health support groups and courses. A great deal of my experience as a counsellor and supervisor came from 11 years working in a mental health charity as a counsellor and counselling service coordinator, managing the counselling service and a team of qualified and trainee counsellors.

I’m a registered and accredited member of the British Association of Counselling and Psychotherapy (BACP). I work as an integrative counsellor, adapting my approach to meet the individual needs of each client. The foundation of my approach is Person Centred Therapy; I also draw from Transactional Analysis (TA) and Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT). I can assist with a variety of issues including, but not limited to, anxiety, depression, low self-esteem, domestic abuse, family conflict, relationship difficulties, bereavement, and workplace stress.

Currently, alongside my private practice, I work as a Counselling Associate for a local organisation providing counselling for employees of local organisations who need support.

I'm qualified to provide counselling supervision for qualified and trainee counsellors. More details can be found in the 'Supervision' section of this website.

Counselling

I will provide a non-judgemental and confidential space to talk. I can help you to explore and find ways to cope with issues, thoughts and emotions that are having negative impact on your life. I will support you to build resilience and confidence as you take steps towards a more fulfilling and happier life. Please see the ‘About me’ page on this website for more details about my experience and the issues I can support you with.

Counselling sessions will last for 50 minutes. In terms of the number of sessions required, there is no 'one size fits all' and this will be regularly reviewed. Depending on the issues brought to counselling, some clients can feel much better after few sessions whereas some clients require more than six sessions to feel more able to cope.

Supervision

I'm a qualified counselling supervisor with a Diploma in Supervision and have experience of supervising qualified and trainee counsellors.

I use the Seven-eyed Model of Supervision (Hawkins and Shohet), enabling an all-encompassing exploration of client sessions and the processes that occur in counselling and supervision.

I will provide a safe space to explore and monitor counselling practice and the supervisory relationship in order to ensure that all work is carried out safely and ethically in accordance with BACP guidelines.

I understand that it can be difficult to find the right supervisor so I'm happy to have a chat to answer any questions you may have.

Privacy Notice

Introduction

Your privacy is very important to me, and I’ll will ensure that your personal information is kept safe and secure. I adhere to current data protection legislation, including the General Data Protection Regulation (EU/2016/679) (the GDPR), the Data Protection Act 2018 and the Privacy and Electronic Communications (EC Directive) Regulations 2003.This privacy notice tells you how and why your information will be processed from the initial contact through to after counselling has ended.

I am happy to answer any questions you have about my data protection policy and can be contacted as follows:

clockworkcounselling.uk@gmail.com

Joanne Lisa Park and Clockwork Counselling are registered as a data controller with the Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO) (Registration number ZB334534)

How I use your information

Initial contact

When you contact me to enquire about counselling or counselling supervision, I will collect and record your name, telephone number and/or email address. Should you decide not to proceed with counselling or counselling supervision, I will ensure that your personal data is deleted within one calendar month. If you would like to delete this information sooner, please let me know.

While you are accessing counselling or counselling supervision

When you begin counselling or counselling supervision, I will collect, record and store your personal information which includes your name, address, contact details and, in respect of counselling, your GP practice. I will also record assessment information, and this will be stored separately under an identifying code.

I will make brief factual notes following each session and these will be stored, using and identifying code. The notes will be stored either digitally with password protection or in a locked filing cabinet.

Everything that you discuss with me is confidential and this confidentiality will only be breached in the following circumstances:

  • Where there is an ethical duty for me to do so, for example when I believe there is a significant risk of serious harm to you or others.
  • Where there is a legal requirement for me to do so, for example a court order.

I will always try to speak to you about this first unless it is considered that this may cause you are others serious harm.

In exceptional circumstances your contact details may be shared with my supervisor in the event that I am incapacitated, so that they can contact you to explain the situation.

To help the counselling sessions run smoothly, your name and contact details will be held digitally on a computer and mobile phone for the purposes of emails and texts. Both devices are password protected. Once counselling has ended, your contact details will be deleted within one calendar month.

After counselling has ended

Once counselling has ended your personal information will be kept for 5 years and then securely destroyed. If you want me to delete your information any sooner, please let me know.

Your Rights

You have the right to ask me to rectify or delete your personal information at any time. I will attend to this as soon as possible upon request.

You have the right to request a copy of your personal information. Please put your request in writing to:

clockworkcounselling.uk@gmail.com

If you have any concerns about how I handle personal information, please do not hesitate to discuss this with me.

If you want to make a formal complaint about the way I have processed your personal information you can contact the Information Commissioners Office (ICO). For more information go to ico.org.uk/make-a-complaint.
